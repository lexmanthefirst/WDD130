NIGERIA

Nigeria, officially the Federal Republic of Nigeria, is a diverse country in western Africa. Covering 356,669 square miles with a population of 222.5 million, it boasts over 250 ethnic groups, including Hausa, Fulani, Yoruba, and Igbo. English and Hausa are the official languages, and Christianity, Islam, and traditional beliefs are prevalent. The economy relies on petroleum, agriculture, and a growing manufacturing sector. Nigeria gained independence in 1960, experienced military coups, and endured a civil war (1967–70). The capital moved to Abuja in 1991, and civilian rule was reinstated in 1999. Challenges persist, including ethnic strife, protests over oil production, and tensions between Muslims and Christians.
